# Observator — Video Voiceover Script

Observator is the UAE's labour market intelligence platform. It brings together government workforce data, live job market feeds, university course catalogs, and AI research into a single decision-making system for policymakers.

Every chart on this screen is backed by verified, traceable data. Nothing estimated. Nothing fabricated.

---

The Supply Side shows the full education pipeline. How many students enroll each year — and how that's changed over two decades.

Which emirates have the most students. The gender balance. The STEM ratio.

Every institution ranked by programs and graduate output. And for the first time — the actual skills each university produces, mapped from course catalogs across more than a hundred institutions.

---

The Demand Side captures the real job market. Monthly hiring trends. Which industries are growing. Which experience levels are in demand.

Salary benchmarks across emirates and occupations. And the skills employers actually require — extracted and standardized from tens of thousands of real job postings.

This is not a survey. This is what the market is doing.

---

The Dashboard is where it all connects. Supply and demand — compared skill by skill.

This chart shows the same skills on both sides — what employers need on the left, what universities teach on the right. The gap between them is the problem to solve.

The heatmap groups skills by category. At a glance, you see where the education system is falling short.

On the left — skills the market demands but nobody teaches. On the right — skills universities invest in but employers don't ask for. This is the misalignment — made visible.

Want to go deeper? Filter by skill, occupation, university, or region. Click any row to drill into the detail — which jobs need this skill, which courses teach it. Switch views — compare institutions, programs, or occupations. The filters work together so you can answer any question.

And every single chart can be flipped to reveal its full data story — how it was calculated, which sources were used, any caveats — with direct links into the Knowledge Base to verify the raw data yourself. Navigate across tables. Follow relationships. Trace any number back to its origin. Full transparency for government-grade decisions.

---

Observator also maps AI disruption risk across the entire economy. This spider chart — modeled after Anthropic's labour market research — shows theoretical AI capability versus what's actually being automated today.

Drill into any job family. See its occupations. See their individual skills. Each skill shows how much AI could affect it.

So workforce planners can design reskilling programs ahead of displacement — not after.

---

And if you'd rather just ask a question — you can. The AI assistant has access to the entire database. Every table. Every view.

Toggle web search for live market intelligence. Every answer grounded in data. Every source cited.

---

A minister sees the gap between education output and market demand. A university president sees which programs align with employer needs. A workforce director sees which occupations need intervention — and which skills to prioritize. Decisions backed by evidence. Not intuition.

---

Observator. See the gap. Close the gap. The UAE labour market — made visible.
